<div class="topic" data-tags="beta blocker heart rate sympathetic angina hypertension dysrhythmia">
<div class="topic-title">1. Beta Blockers</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">What do they do?</div><div class="a">Block beta receptors → <span class="red">slow heart rate · improve rhythmicity · reduce vessel constriction</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Used for?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Hypertension · angina · dysrhythmia · migraines · anxiety</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="cardiac glycosides digitalis digoxin congestive heart failure sodium potassium calcium">
<div class="topic-title">1. Cardiac Glycosides</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">MOA?</div><div class="a">Alter <span class="red">Na-K pump → influx of Ca2+</span> → increases force of contraction · slows AV node → reduces heart rate</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Used for?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Congestive heart failure · dysrhythmias</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="vasodilator nitroglycerin cgmp nitrous oxide angina">
<div class="topic-title">1. Vasodilators</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">MOA?</div><div class="a">Metabolized to <span class="red">nitrous oxide (NO)</span> → increases <span class="red">cGMP</span> → vasodilation · relief in <span class="red">2–3 minutes</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Used for?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Angina · hypertension · CHF</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="ace inhibitor angiotensin renin hypertension cough">
<div class="topic-title">1. ACE Inhibitors</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">MOA?</div><div class="a">Block ACE enzyme → prevents <span class="red">angiotensin I → angiotensin II</span> (vasoconstrictor) · also reduces aldosterone</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Side effect?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Dry continuous cough</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="calcium channel blocker edema ankles tachycardia migraine">
<div class="topic-title">1. Calcium Channel Blockers</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">MOA?</div><div class="a">Block <span class="red">Ca2+ influx</span> into cardiac/vascular smooth muscle → reduces rate + force of contraction + vasodilation</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Side effect?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Edema in feet/ankles</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="anti seizure gaba benzodiazepine valium diazepam hyperpolarization chloride">
<div class="topic-title">2. Anti-Seizure Drugs</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">3 mechanisms?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">1.</span> Alter cell membranes (ion permeability) · <span class="red">2.</span> Enhance GABA → Cl- influx → hyperpolarization · <span class="red">3.</span> Block Na+ or Ca2+ channels</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Benzodiazepines MOA?</div><div class="a">Enhance <span class="red">GABA → Cl- influx → hyperpolarization</span> → neurons harder to activate</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="half life metabolism elimination liver kidney first pass bioavailability">
<div class="topic-title">3. Half-Life</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Define?</div><div class="a">Time to reduce blood drug concentration to <span class="red">50%</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Example: 100mg, 20-min half-life — after 40 min?</div><div class="a">20 min = <span class="red">50mg</span> · 40 min = <span class="red">25mg</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Key organs?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Liver</span> = metabolism · <span class="red">Kidneys</span> = elimination</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">First-Pass Effect?</div><div class="a">Drug absorbed orally → <span class="red">liver metabolizes it FIRST</span> before rest of body → reduces bioavailability · sublingual/IV/IM bypass this</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="cancer hormonal mitotic spindle alkylating angiogenesis interferon dna cell cycle">
<div class="topic-title">4. Cancer Drugs</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Hormonal agents?</div><div class="a">Prostate cancer → <span class="red">estrogenic hormones</span> (antagonize testosterone) · Breast cancer → <span class="red">androgenic hormones</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Mitotic spindle drugs?</div><div class="a">Tubulin inhibitors · break down spindle during <span class="red">M phase</span> → cell CANNOT divide</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Alkylating drugs?</div><div class="a">Effective ANY phase · <span class="red">crosslink DNA strands</span> → DNA cannot replicate → cell dies</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Angiogenesis inhibitors?</div><div class="a">Block <span class="red">Tumor Angiogenesis Factor (TAF)</span> → tumor cannot get blood supply → starved</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Interferons?</div><div class="a">Stimulate <span class="red">T killer cells + macrophages</span> · Alpha (leukocytes) · Beta (fibroblasts) · Gamma (T lymphocytes)</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Corticosteroids enter nucleus and do what?</div><div class="a">Bind glucocorticoid receptor → enters <span class="red">nucleus → DNA transcription → mRNA</span> → proteins produced → anti-inflammatory effects</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="antihistamine cromolyn beta adrenergic xanthine anticholinergic bronchodilation asthma photosensitivity">
<div class="topic-title">5. Respiratory Drugs</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Antihistamines?</div><div class="a">Block <span class="red">H1 + H2 receptor sites</span> · some cross BBB → drowsiness</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Cromolyn Sodium?</div><div class="a">Stabilizes <span class="red">mast cell membranes</span> → prevents histamine release · <span class="red">PREVENTIVE ONLY</span> · not for acute attacks</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Beta-Adrenergic Agonists?</div><div class="a">Mimic norepinephrine on <span class="red">beta-2 receptors</span> → bronchodilation · best via <span class="red">inhalation</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Xanthine Derivatives?</div><div class="a">Stimulate <span class="red">bronchodilation</span> · inhibit smooth muscle contraction · inhibit prostaglandin formation</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Anticholinergic drugs?</div><div class="a">Block <span class="red">ACh receptor sites</span> in bronchial tree → reduce bronchoconstriction → bronchodilation</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Drugs causing photosensitivity?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Antibiotics · muscle relaxants · asthma meds · NSAIDs · diuretics · antidepressants</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="injection excipient depot short acting long acting wait time discontinuation intra articular patch">
<div class="topic-title">6. Injections & Treatment</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Excipients — what are they?</div><div class="a">Inactive substances in oral meds · <span class="red">1. Keep drug stable on shelf · 2. Help drug dissolve in GI tract</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">5 injection routes?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Intradermal · Subcutaneous · Intramuscular · Intravenous · Intra-articular</span> (up to 6 weeks)</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Short-acting injection — wait time?</div><div class="a">Can include site after <span class="red">24–48 hours</span> (e.g. vaccine)</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">After drug discontinuation — focused tissue work?</div><div class="a">Minimum <span class="red">10 DAYS</span> after discontinuation</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Long-acting injection — RMT rule?</div><div class="a">Massage over site is <span class="red">CONTRAINDICATED</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Skin patch — RMT rule?</div><div class="a">Maintain <span class="red">4–6 inch radius</span> · NEVER massage over it · NEVER remove it</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Intra-articular adverse reactions?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Excess immune reaction → inflammation</span> · articular cartilage damage with repeated injections</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="pharmacology pharmacy pharmacognosy pharmacodynamics pharmacokinetics definitions">
<div class="topic-title">7. Pharmacology Definitions</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Pharmacology?</div><div class="a">Study of action of chemicals on living organisms</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Pharmacy?</div><div class="a">Science of preparing and dispensing medications</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Pharmacognosy?</div><div class="a">Study of medicines from <span class="red">natural sources</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Pharmacodynamics?</div><div class="a">What the <span class="red">DRUG does to the BODY</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Pharmacokinetics?</div><div class="a">What the <span class="red">BODY does to the DRUG</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">3 drug effects?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">1.</span> Therapeutic · <span class="red">2.</span> Side effects/Adverse · <span class="red">3.</span> Unpredictable (allergic OR idiosyncratic)</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Exercise intolerance signs?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Breathing difficulty · palpitations · chest pain · dizziness · nausea · light-headedness</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="aspirin cox cyclooxygenase prostaglandin thromboxane stomach kidney gastric">
<div class="topic-title">8. Aspirin — MOA</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Full MOA?</div><div class="a">Irritated cells → release arachidonic acid → <span class="red">COX enzyme</span> converts it → prostaglandins + thromboxanes · Aspirin <span class="red">INHIBITS COX</span> → blocks both</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Why stomach irritation?</div><div class="a">Prostaglandins also <span class="red">protect gastric mucosa</span> + support kidney function · blocking = gastric irritation + kidney dysfunction</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Why thins blood?</div><div class="a">Thromboxane A2 = platelet aggregation · Aspirin blocks it → <span class="red">no platelet clumping · 1 week effect</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="addisons corticosteroid thrombus fibrin plasmin thromboxane blood clot">
<div class="topic-title">9. Key Definitions</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Addison's Disease treatment?</div><div class="a">Adrenal glands fail → treated with <span class="red">PHYSIOLOGIC DOSE</span> of corticosteroids (replaces daily cortisol) · hormone REPLACEMENT strategy</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Corticosteroids — effects on inflammation + immunity?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Anti-inflammatory</span>: suppress prostaglandins, leukotrienes, histamine · <span class="red">Immunosuppressant</span>: inhibit WBCs + macrophages · <span class="red">Catabolic</span>: break down muscle, tendons, bones, ligaments, articular cartilage</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Thrombus?</div><div class="a">Clot-like plug from <span class="red">platelets activated inside a blood vessel</span> · platelets + fibrin + RBCs</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Fibrin?</div><div class="a">Protein forming <span class="red">structural mesh of a blood clot</span> · fibrinogen → converted by thrombin → fibrin</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Plasmin?</div><div class="a">Enzyme that <span class="red">breaks down fibrin mesh</span> (dissolves clots) · thrombolytics promote its formation</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Thromboxane?</div><div class="a">Thromboxane A2 = <span class="red">platelet aggregation + vasoconstriction</span> · aspirin/NSAIDs inhibit it</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="mucous membrane depot topical parenteral time released enteric coated sublingual patch transdermal">
<div class="topic-title">10. Drug Applications</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Mucous Membranes?</div><div class="a">Internal surfaces exposed to outside (digestive, respiratory, reproductive, urinary) · example: <span class="red">sublingual nitroglycerin → minutes</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Depot?</div><div class="a">Long-acting formula · contraceptives = <span class="red">up to 5 years</span> · corticosteroid joint depot = <span class="red">6 weeks</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Topicals — 3 types?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">1.</span> Local skin conditions · <span class="red">2.</span> Muscle/joint pain (counter-irritants → cool then warm) · <span class="red">3.</span> Systemic (transdermal patches)</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Parenteral?</div><div class="a">By INJECTION · bypasses GI · <span class="red">IV = 100% bioavailability</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Time-Released?</div><div class="a">Portion dissolves immediately · rest dissolves gradually → <span class="red">constant steady drug level</span> all day</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Drugs adjunct to anesthesia (prevent spasms)?</div><div class="a">NMJ blockers (succinylcholine) → inhibit ACh → <span class="red">temporary FLACCID PARALYSIS</span> → no spasms/reflexes during surgery</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="type 1 type 2 diabetes insulin hypoglycemia hyperglycemia blood glucose">
<div class="topic-title">11. Type 1 vs Type 2 Diabetes</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Type 1?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">5–10%</span> of cases · pancreas CEASES insulin production · autoimmune · childhood · <span class="red">always needs exogenous insulin</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Type 2?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">90–95%</span> of cases · insulin produced but <span class="red">cells RESIST it</span> · managed with lifestyle + oral drugs</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Hypoglycemia signs?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Sweating · shakiness · pallor · tachycardia · confusion · moist pale skin</span> · RAPID onset</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Hyperglycemia signs?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Thirst · frequent urination · acetone breath · dry flushed skin</span> · slower onset</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">RMT emergency rule?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">ALWAYS GIVE SUGAR — NEVER GIVE INSULIN</span> · fruit juice, candy · no improvement in 5–10 min = call help</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Normal blood glucose?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">80–90 mg/100 mL</span></div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="narcotic opioid skeletal muscle relaxant corticosteroid nsaid spasm spasticity">
<div class="topic-title">12. Pain & Inflammation Drugs</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Narcotic analgesics MOA?</div><div class="a">Mimic <span class="red">endorphins/enkephalins</span> → opioid receptors · alter <span class="red">Ca2+ + K+ flow</span> · inhibit <span class="red">cAMP</span> → interrupts pain signals</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Spasm vs Spasticity?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">SPASM</span>: high muscle tone · protective/reflexive · treated with CENTRALLY acting relaxants · <span class="red">SPASTICITY</span>: CNS injury (MS, stroke, SCI, CP) · loss of inhibitory input · hypertonicity, clonus, exaggerated DTRs · treated with PERIPHERALLY acting relaxants</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">NSAIDs — 4 properties?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Anti-inflammatory · analgesic · antipyretic · anticoagulant</span> · NOTE: acetaminophen has NO anti-inflammatory or anticoagulant effect</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Corticosteroids RMT alert?</div><div class="a">Avoid stress on muscles/bones/joints · osteoporosis risk · <span class="red">reschedule if YOU are unwell</span> — patient is immunocompromised</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="loop diuretics thiazide potassium sparing nephron tubule henle collecting duct">
<div class="topic-title">13. Diuretics</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Thiazide — where + MOA?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Distal convoluted tubule</span> · block Na+ and Cl- resorption · water follows via osmosis</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Loop — where + MOA?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Ascending Loop of Henle</span> · inhibit Cl- resorption · most <span class="red">POTENT</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Potassium-sparing — where + MOA?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Collecting duct</span> · conserve K+ · less potent · prevents hypokalemia</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">RMT alert?</div><div class="a">Electrolyte imbalance → cramping · palpitations = low K+ → refer · causes hyperglycemia · photosensitivity</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="spasm paralysis muscle tone flaccid spasticity centrally peripherally acting NMJ">
<div class="topic-title">14. Spasm / Paralysis / Muscle Tone</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Muscle Spasm?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">HIGH increase in tone</span> · protective OR reflexive · centrally acting relaxants</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Spasticity?</div><div class="a">CNS injury → loss of inhibitory input · <span class="red">hypertonicity · clonus · exaggerated DTRs · scissoring · fixed joints</span> · peripherally acting relaxants</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Flaccid Paralysis?</div><div class="a">NMJ blockers (succinylcholine) → inhibit ACh → <span class="red">complete loss of tone + voluntary movement</span> · temporary · used in surgery</div></div>
</div>

<div class="topic" data-tags="sulfonylurea biguanide metformin alpha glucosidase thiazolidinedione oral diabetic hypoglycemia B12">
<div class="topic-title">15. Oral Diabetic Drugs</div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Sulfonylureas?</div><div class="a">Type 2 only · <span class="red">stimulate insulin SECRETION from beta cells</span> · RISK: rapid hypoglycemia · DANGEROUS with NSAIDs → insulin shock</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Biguanides (Metformin)?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">1. Decrease hepatic glucose · 2. Decrease intestinal absorption · 3. Increase peripheral glucose uptake</span> · NO hypoglycemia · NO weight gain · side effect: <span class="red">B12 depletion</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Alpha-Glucosidase Inhibitors?</div><div class="a">In intestines · inhibit alpha-glucosidase enzyme → <span class="red">delayed rise in blood glucose after meals</span> · side effect: GI disturbances</div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Thiazolidinediones?</div><div class="a">Act on <span class="red">PPAR-gamma receptors</span> → increase insulin sensitivity · side effect: <span class="red">edema</span></div></div>
<div class="item"><div class="q">Quick summary?</div><div class="a"><span class="red">Sulfonylureas</span> = release insulin · <span class="red">Biguanides</span> = reduce hepatic glucose · <span class="red">Alpha-glucosidase</span> = delay absorption · <span class="red">Thiazolidinediones</span> = increase sensitivity</div></div>
</div>
